For our project we wanted to create a macroscopic physics engine that could realisticly visualize collision behavior and energy transfer. 
In our final program we decided to visualize interactions between colliding pendulums, and how kinetic and potential energy are exchanged across bodies. This setup highlights emergent behaviors such as momenmtum transfer, damping effects, and nonlinear motion from repeating interactions.

At the core is the Pendulum abstraction, which is composed of two primary elements: a WreckingBall and a Chain.
- The WreckingBall class encapsulates the physical properties of the pendulum bob, including mass, gravitational acceleration, and air resistance. It is responsible for computing forces acting on the bob and updating its motion over time using numerical integration.
- The Chain class models the constraint system connecting the wrecking ball to a fixed anchor point. It maintains the pendulum’s length and enforces motion constraints by tracking tension forces and approximating spring-like behavior along discrete chain links. This enables more realistic motion compared to a rigid rod approximation, particularly under dynamic loading conditions.
Collision handling is managed through the Block class, which detects and resolves interactions between pendulum bobs. In the current implementation, it is used to monitor collisions near rest positions and during oscillation, applying appropriate response calculations to conserve momentum and simulate energy transfer. The design of this class is generalizable and can be extended to support collisions between arbitrary rigid bodies beyond pendulum systems.
The BlockTower class acts as the system-level controller, maintaining a collection of pendulums and managing their spatial configuration. It defines anchor points for each chain, initializes system geometry, and coordinates updates across all components during simulation steps.

Development was distributed across team members: Advaiith implemented the ChainLink and Chain classes, focusing on constraint modeling and force propagation; Steven developed the WreckingBall class, including force calculations and motion integration; Harris implemented the BlockTower and Block classes, handling system organization and collision detection/response.
